About Us!!

The Independent Corrupt Practices and Other Related Offences Commission (ICPC) is a Nigerian government agency established to combat corruption and related offenses. Its mandate includes the following:

  • Prevention: ICPC is responsible for preventing corruption by sensitizing the public and educating them about the negative impacts of corruption, as well as promoting ethical conduct and good governance.

  • Investigation and Prosecution: ICPC has the authority to investigate and prosecute cases of corruption and related offenses, such as bribery, fraud, embezzlement, and abuse of office. It can also recover proceeds of corruption and initiate legal proceedings against offenders.

  • Enforcement: ICPC has the power to enforce compliance with ethical standards and codes of conduct in public and private organizations. It can conduct systems study and review to identify areas vulnerable to corruption and make recommendations for improvement.

  • Asset Recovery: ICPC is responsible for tracing, seizing, and recovering assets acquired through corrupt practices. This includes freezing and confiscating assets obtained through corruption, money laundering, or other related offenses.

  • Collaboration: ICPC collaborates with other law enforcement agencies, both within Nigeria and internationally, to exchange information, intelligence, and expertise in the fight against corruption. It also works with civil society organizations and the media to promote transparency and accountability.
